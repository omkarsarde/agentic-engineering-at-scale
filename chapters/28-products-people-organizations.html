28.3 Design trust through legible state and receipts

An expense-triage agent reaches launch review with green technical dashboards. Its task benchmark improved, latency meets the objective, effect authorization is enforced, and the review queue stays within its service target. The meeting approves expansion in eleven minutes. Six weeks later, finance cannot show that reimbursements close faster. New reviewers accept bad categories much more often than experienced reviewers. Screen-reader users abandon the streaming review flow. The product owner assumed the risk team would request recertification after a prompt change; the risk team assumed the platform would do it. Nobody owned the whole decision.

This is not one missing metric. It is a missing socio-technical system: the product, its users and affected people, the humans who review or repair it, the teams who change it, and the management process that decides whether its evidence still justifies its authority. Chapter 27 keeps the service operating and runs queues and incidents. This chapter asks a different question: should this product exist in this form, for these people, under this organization, at this autonomy level?

Treat a launch argument as three nested rings. The user ring asks whether a person can form an accurate model of what the agent is doing and intervene. The product ring asks whether use causes a worthwhile outcome compared with a simpler baseline. The organization ring asks whether named owners can maintain the claim through changes. A release is not ready when one ring is green; it is ready only when their evidence supports the same bounded decision.

28.3.1 Concern boundaries are product architecture

An agent product needs more than a chat surface. Separate at least six concerns: goal elicitation, planning, execution, evidence, intervention, and outcome confirmation. Goal elicitation turns an underspecified request into a bounded objective and names what must not happen. Planning exposes consequential interpretation before effects. Execution invokes models and tools under the Chapter 17 harness. Evidence distinguishes source facts, model inferences, unresolved assumptions, and effect receipts. Intervention lets a person correct, pause, deny, or narrow authority. Outcome confirmation checks the external state that mattered.

This separation is the product counterpart of mixed-initiative interaction. The person and automation can each take initiative, but initiative should move according to expected value, uncertainty, cost of interruption, and consequence—not because the agent always asks or always acts. A low-consequence, reversible formatting choice may proceed silently. A novel payee, uncertain policy interpretation, or irreversible submission should surface a decision point with the exact delta.

Before an action, show a plan diff: what the agent intends to change, from which current state to which proposed state, with affected objects and permissions. Afterward, show a receipt: the authoritative system, operation identity, committed result, timestamp, and reconciliation status. A prose message saying “done” is not a receipt. Nor should a product expose private chain-of-thought as an explanation. Give users the decision-relevant evidence—sources, assumptions, uncertainty, rule or tool result, and action record—without claiming that a generated rationale faithfully reveals internal computation. Chapter 25 owns that distinction.

Use stable epistemic labels. “Source fact,” “user-provided,” “system state,” “model inference,” “estimate,” and “unresolved” should retain the same meaning across screens and modalities. Calibrated numeric probabilities are useful only when validated for the task and slice. Otherwise, concrete statements such as “two required receipts are missing” are more actionable than ornamental confidence. A refusal should name the boundary and a safe next step; an uncertainty should identify what new evidence could resolve it.

Personalization raises both usefulness and risk. Make remembered preferences inspectable, scoped, editable, exportable when appropriate, and resettable. Distinguish a temporary session choice from a durable profile and an organizational policy. Never infer a sensitive preference merely because it predicts clicks. Reset must remove the preference from every product surface that consumes it, while Chapter 18 owns deletion lineage through derived memory artifacts.

28.4 What you will build

TipOne integrated launch-review artifact

You will build launch_metrics.py around one candidate expense-report triage agent. A compact fixture expands into a 500-row reviewer decision log and a 500-row usage ledger. The artifact computes the reliance matrix by cohort, the invocation-to-outcome funnel, cost per realized outcome, a review-time break-even point, an explicitly bounded energy intensity, and four predeclared launch conditions. It attaches those results to one AIMS risk-register entry and returns SHIP or HOLD_FULL_LAUNCH.

The fixture is synthetic so the decision is reproducible. Its intended tension is realistic: the candidate beats the conventional workflow on cost, yet fails overall appropriate reliance, cohort overreliance, and assistive-technology completion. The permitted next step is therefore a read-only pilot for experienced reviewers—not a vote to ignore the failing slices.

28.5 Measure appropriate reliance, not engagement

Trust is a person’s attitude that an agent will help achieve a goal under uncertainty. Reliance is observable behavior: accepting, rejecting, checking, delegating, or intervening. A product cannot directly optimize “trust” as a scalar without inviting manipulation. It can design information and control so that reliance tracks demonstrated capability.

The target is appropriate reliance, not maximal acceptance. For a set of recommendations with externally established correctness, classify each decision into four cells:

Recommendation Person accepts Person rejects
correct correct accept, (N_{CA}) correct reject, (N_{CR})
wrong wrong accept, (N_{WA}) wrong reject, (N_{WR})

Define

R_{appropriate}=\frac{N_{CA}+N_{WR}}{N}, \qquad R_{over}=\frac{N_{WA}}{N_{WA}+N_{WR}}, \qquad R_{under}=\frac{N_{CR}}{N_{CA}+N_{CR}}.

The first rate measures agreement with correctness. The second asks how often a person accepts when the agent is wrong; the third asks how often useful recommendations are rejected. Report all three. A single “agreement with AI” number makes correct acceptance and harmful wrong acceptance look alike. Likewise, acceptance, daily active use, time in product, and satisfaction can rise while judgment worsens.

Correctness needs an outcome-grounded label. Reviewer agreement alone is circular: if every reviewer accepts the same bad recommendation, agreement is high and reliance is poor. Use authoritative downstream state, independently adjudicated cases, or a delayed outcome whose limitations are recorded. Confirmed catches are especially informative: did a rejection prevent an actual defect, or merely create rework?

The artifact keeps the four cells intact rather than reducing them too early:

# launch_metrics.py — one reliance slice
def _reliance_rates(rows):
    rows = list(rows)
    counts = Counter(row["quadrant"] for row in rows)
    correct = counts["correct_accept"] + counts["correct_reject"]
    wrong = counts["wrong_accept"] + counts["wrong_reject"]
    catches = sum(row["confirmed_catch"] for row in rows)
    return {
        "n": len(rows),
        "quadrants": {name: counts[name] for name in QUADRANTS},
        "appropriate_rate": round(
            (counts["correct_accept"] + counts["wrong_reject"]) / len(rows), 4
        ),
        "overreliance_rate": round(counts["wrong_accept"] / wrong, 4),
        "underreliance_rate": round(counts["correct_reject"] / correct, 4),
        "confirmed_catch_rate": round(catches / counts["wrong_reject"], 4),
    }

The aggregate appropriate rate in Figure 28.2 is (441/500=0.882). More importantly, the new-reviewer slice accepts 25 of 30 wrong recommendations: an overreliance rate of (0.833). An average can hide exactly the population that a new rollout creates. Slice by experience, language, assistive technology, task novelty, consequence, time pressure, and any protected or operational group justified by the context. Predeclare minimum sample handling; do not publish unstable rankings of individual reviewers.

Interventions have trade-offs. Showing sources and exact action deltas can improve discrimination. Requiring a person to make an initial judgment before seeing the recommendation, request evidence, or explain a choice can reduce overreliance, but also adds effort and can reduce satisfaction. Warnings shown on every case become wallpaper. Confidence displays can anchor users when calibration is poor. Test interventions on all four cells, decision time, comprehension, accessibility, downstream defects, and distributional effects. The best interface is not the one users like most in isolation; it is the one that helps the relevant people accept good assistance and catch bad assistance at a sustainable cost.

28.6 Make accessibility part of system correctness

Accessibility is not a compliance pass added after model quality. It is whether people with varied sensory, motor, cognitive, language, and situational needs can perceive state, understand consequences, operate controls, recover from error, and obtain the same outcome. For an agent, inaccessible state is also a control defect: a person cannot supervise a plan or stop an action they cannot perceive.

Start with the ordinary web and software baseline: semantic structure; keyboard operation; visible focus; sufficient contrast; text alternatives; captions and transcripts; labels and instructions; predictable navigation; zoom and reflow; error identification; and compatibility with assistive technologies. Then test the interaction patterns agents add:

  • Streaming output: do not move focus on every token or flood a live region. Announce meaningful completion states and offer pause, replay, or a non-streaming view.
  • Plans and tool calls: represent state in semantic text, not only animation, color, indentation, or a canvas. A collapsed detail must expose whether an effect is pending.
  • Approval: focus the exact proposed change, its evidence, and distinct approve, deny, modify, and escalate controls. Preserve enough time to decide; refresh stale state before effect.
  • Voice and multimodality: provide text alternatives, captions, transcripts, and a path that does not require speech, hearing, precise pointing, or camera use.
  • Generated interface elements: constrain them to a tested component system. A model should not invent unlabeled controls or change keyboard order at runtime.
  • Errors and uncertainty: identify the field or claim, state what happened, preserve entered work, and give a correction path without requiring memory of a vanished message.

Conformance to a published accessibility standard is a floor, not evidence that the agent task works. Combine automated checks, manual keyboard and screen-reader testing, zoom and reflow tests, cognitive walkthroughs, and representative task studies. Automated scanners catch only classes of detectable violations. Include disabled people and assistive-technology users in discovery and evaluation, compensate expertise, and do not make one participant speak for a population.

Measure the same funnel and outcome by access mode. In the fixture, 85 assistive-technology users invoke the agent but only 65 complete the recommendation step, an adjacent conversion of (65/85=0.765). The predeclared floor is (0.85). Shipping because the overall completion count is acceptable would treat exclusion as a rounding error. The correct engineering response is to diagnose the interaction, repair it, and repeat the task—not lower the cohort gate after looking at the result.

Language and culture require the same seriousness. Translation quality, reading level, names, addresses, dates, currencies, formality, and policy concepts can change both comprehension and model behavior. Evaluate the end-to-end task with people who use the language in context. A fluent response is not necessarily an accurate or culturally suitable action. Give users control over language and locale; do not silently infer them from sensitive proxies.

Accessibility ownership must survive procurement. If a vendor supplies the model or interface, the product team still needs acceptance criteria, test access, remediation terms, change notification, and a fallback. A vendor conformance report is evidence about a named version and scope, not a transferable guarantee for your prompts, generated content, wrapper, tools, or workflow.

28.7 Discover value before adding autonomy

Agents are attractive when the path to an outcome is variable, information-rich, and partly judgmental. Those same properties make them expensive and difficult to evaluate. Begin with the least agentic baseline capable of producing value: better instructions, search, a form, a deterministic rule, a conventional workflow, or a single model call. If the baseline solves the problem, added autonomy is complexity without value.

Write a product hypothesis in causal language:

For a named population performing a named job, this bounded agent intervention will improve a measurable outcome relative to a named baseline, without violating reliance, accessibility, safety, labor, or cost conditions.

“Users want an AI assistant” is not a hypothesis. Neither are benchmark score, tokens generated, tool calls, chat sessions, or minutes spent. A support agent may aim to increase correctly resolved cases without repeat contact. A coding agent may reduce accepted-change lead time without increasing escaped defects. An expense agent may reduce time to correctly settled reimbursement without increasing improper categorization.

Use qualitative discovery before automation: observe the job, artifacts, workarounds, handoffs, exceptions, and sources of truth. Interview affected people as well as the purchaser and operator. Map who benefits, who does extra work, who absorbs errors, who can refuse, and who can appeal. The apparent “manual bottleneck” may be a necessary negotiation, duty separation, or missing upstream data rather than an opportunity to make a model decide faster.

Prototype the information and decision flow before the autonomous loop. A concierge test, Wizard-of-Oz study, read-only copilot, or offline replay can reveal whether recommendations are actionable and which context is missing. Instrument the baseline first. Without its outcome rate, time, defect, labor, and cost, the launch review cannot know whether the agent improved anything.

Declare kill criteria before the pilot. Each condition needs a metric, population or slice, threshold, measurement window, owner, and response. Include value and non-negotiable constraints. For example: hold write authority if appropriate reliance is below 0.90, if any adequately sampled cohort accepts more than 25 percent of wrong recommendations, if assistive-technology completion is below 0.85, or if cost per realized outcome exceeds the conventional workflow. A threshold set after viewing results is an explanation, not a gate.

Offline evaluation remains necessary but insufficient. Chapter 22 establishes task quality, slice performance, judge validity, uncertainty, and powered comparisons. Product discovery asks whether invoking the system changes a real outcome. Use randomized experiments where treatment assignment and interference assumptions are credible. Otherwise use a defensible quasi-experiment, phased rollout, matched comparison, interrupted time series, or carefully bounded before/after study, and state the confounders. Never attribute every post-launch change to the agent.

Selection effects matter. Early adopters may have easier tasks or more enthusiasm. People invoke assistance selectively, so “outcome among invoked cases” does not estimate “effect if offered” without assumptions. Novelty can temporarily inflate engagement and diligence; later, reviewers may automate their attention. Track assigned, exposed, invoked, completed, and retained populations, and preserve a contemporaneous baseline long enough to see learning and fatigue.

28.8 Follow the invocation-to-outcome funnel

A product dashboard often begins at invocation because that is where the agent emits an event. The causal product story begins earlier and ends later. Define a nested funnel appropriate to the job:

  1. eligible or exposed: the person and task could use the intervention;
  2. invoked: the person or policy actually starts it;
  3. completed: the agent returns a usable candidate;
  4. accepted or edited: the candidate enters the human or system workflow;
  5. outcome realized: authoritative downstream evidence confirms the intended result;
  6. retained: the outcome or repeated use remains useful after a meaningful interval.

For counts (n_0,,n_K), adjacent conversion is (c_k=n_k/n_{k-1}), while end-to-end conversion is (n_K/n_0). Keep both. End-to-end value can fall even when model completion improves because invocation, review, integration, or downstream realization worsens.

    A -->|"−35"| O["Outcome realized<br/>345"]

Figure 28.3 prevents an inference that 460 invocations equal 460 outcomes. Every stage needs an event definition, identity join, time window, and source of truth. A generated answer is completion; it is not a resolved case. A proposed expense category is not a settled reimbursement. Chapter 27’s usage and effect ledgers provide operational records; this chapter maps them to product outcomes.

Instrument edits, rejection, rework, escalation, delayed reversal, appeal, and downstream harm. Edits are not simply failures: they may be the intended joint-work pattern. But copying the recommendation into another system can look like acceptance even when the user corrects it later. Join to authoritative state and define attribution windows.

Review labor is a hidden funnel stage and a hidden cost. Count reading, evidence retrieval, correction, escalation, queue wait, training, quality sampling, and downstream repair. Separate queue wait from active decision time; Chapter 27 owns staffing and queue mechanics. Here, the question is whether that labor creates enough discrimination and value to justify the product design. If almost every case receives a costly approval, compare the system with a deterministic policy that automatically handles the safe subset.

Experiment at the unit where treatment is assigned. A user-level assignment avoids a person switching interfaces, but team members may share outputs. A case-level assignment increases power but can leak learning across conditions. Organizational rollouts have few clusters and strong time trends. Predeclare the primary outcome, guardrails, assignment unit, sample-size logic, stopping rule, and novelty window. Use Chapter 22 for interval estimates and multiple comparisons rather than manufacturing significance inside a launch dashboard.

Selection into invocation should itself become a metric. Compare exposed non-invokers with invokers, record why tasks are ineligible, and sample abandoned paths. When the hardest cases avoid the tool, high success among invoked cases may be true and irrelevant. When only novices invoke it, the same aggregate may conceal a dependence risk. Product telemetry is not a substitute for the assignment and population diagram.

28.9 Price outcomes, labor, and energy

Return on investment begins with a counterfactual: what would the organization and affected people spend, receive, and risk without this product? Use the conventional workflow as the baseline, not zero. The relevant denominator is a realized, quality-qualified outcome.

For a measurement window, define

C_{outcome}=\frac{C_{inference}+C_{retrieval}+C_{review}+C_{remediation}+C_{platform}}{N_{qualified\ outcomes}}.

The numerator includes failed and abandoned attempts attributable to the population. Per conversation pricing can hide multi-call loops; per-token pricing can hide human review; infrastructure allocation can hide idle capacity; labor estimates can hide interruptions and training. Chapter 26 owns the metered journey ledger. This chapter adds product labor and divides by the outcome.

The fixture prices 500 exposures at 0.09 machine cost units each, 31,000 review seconds at 36 units per hour, and 34 wrong accepts at 4 remediation units each. Total cost is 491 units for 345 realized outcomes, or 1.4232 per outcome, below the 1.60 baseline. That is a real pass—and still insufficient to launch because three other conditions fail.

Review time has a useful break-even threshold. Let (C_b) be baseline cost per outcome, (N_o) realized outcomes, (C_{nr}) all non-review cost, (r_h) reviewer cost per second, and (N_r) reviewed cases. The maximum average review duration compatible with the baseline is

t_{max}=\frac{C_bN_o-C_{nr}}{r_hN_r}.

The fixture’s average is 62.0 seconds and break-even is 74.2 seconds. This threshold helps a team see how interface changes or harder cohorts affect economics. It is not a target to rush people toward. Shorter decisions can increase wrong acceptance; evaluate time with reliance and outcome.

Outcome-based commercial pricing can align a vendor with a customer only when “outcome” is attributable, quality-qualified, deduplicated, reversible when later invalidated, and protected against gaming. Specify eligibility, attribution window, dispute process, exclusions, evidence source, and who pays for retries, escalations, human labor, and harms. A vendor should not be paid for deflecting a case that returns unresolved. Nor should a customer demand an outcome price while withholding the authoritative label.

Economic value is distributed. Record whose time is saved, whose workload grows, who bears errors, and whether a faster process transfers unpaid correction to users or reviewers. Include training, accessibility remediation, governance, procurement, security, incident recovery, and exit costs. A narrow departmental ROI can be negative for the organization or public.

Energy intensity needs the same denominator and an explicit measurement boundary:

E_{outcome}=\frac{E_{accelerator}+E_{host}+E_{idle\ allocation}+E_{facility}+E_{network?}}{N_{qualified\ outcomes}}.

The question mark is deliberate: different measurements include different components. State whether energy is measured or estimated, average or marginal, which hardware and region, allocation of idle capacity, power-usage effectiveness, request shape, model and precision, cache behavior, retries, network, and user devices. Do not compare two published numbers with different boundaries. Carbon and water additionally require location and time-sensitive factors; model development and hardware life-cycle impacts are different scopes from serving.

The artifact reports a synthetic allocated serving value of 0.2609 watt-hours per realized outcome and explicitly excludes user devices. It is useful for regression tests within one fixed boundary, not a claim about a vendor or a universal agent query. The highest-leverage optimization is often product-level: avoid valueless invocations, use a smaller sufficient workflow, cache safely, reduce retries, and increase qualified outcomes—not merely shorten one prompt.

Professional responsibility constrains the business case. Engineers should disclose material limitations, uncertainty, conflicts, affected populations, and evidence gaps; protect confidentiality; avoid deceptive claims; and prioritize public and user welfare. Refuse a launch narrative that relabels a failing slice as “edge cases” without analysis. Escalation paths and protected dissent are system controls, not cultural luxuries.

28.10 Organize for staged autonomy

An agent product crosses application, model, data, platform, security, risk, accessibility, domain, operations, and legal boundaries. A committee containing all of them does not automatically create ownership. Assign one stream-aligned product team end-to-end responsibility for the user job, outcome, evaluation, interaction, and release. Give it a paved road from an AI platform team: gateway, model catalog, identity, policy enforcement, evaluation infrastructure, tracing, durable execution, and release evidence. Use enabling specialists to help teams acquire accessibility, safety, data, and domain capabilities rather than becoming permanent ticket queues.

Centralize mechanisms that must be consistent; keep product judgments near the work. The platform can enforce identity, immutable bundle versions, effect policy, and required evidence fields. It cannot decide the semantic cost of a wrong medical summary or whether expense reviewers understand a policy exception. A risk function sets minimum governance, independently challenges high-consequence claims, and can block; it should not become the unnamed owner of product outcomes.

Build-versus-buy is a responsibility allocation, not a binary architecture choice. Compare strategic differentiation, data and residency, customization, latency, scale, reliability, portability, observability, security controls, accessibility evidence, model-change notice, evaluation access, incident duties, price exposure, staffing, and exit cost. For every purchased layer, record:

Responsibility Vendor evidence Product-team duty
model behavior version/change notice, evaluation access, known limits task and slice evaluation in the actual scaffold
data handling contract, subprocessors, retention and region controls minimization, configuration, lineage, deletion, verification
availability service objectives and incident channel degraded product contract and business continuity
accessibility scoped conformance evidence and remediation process end-to-end task testing in the wrapper
security control reports, vulnerability and incident terms threat model, least privilege, effect enforcement
lifecycle deprecation and export terms migration rehearsal, portable data and prompts, exit owner

You can outsource operation; you cannot outsource accountability for how the integrated product is used.

A golden path is the supported route from prototype to production with sensible defaults, examples, templates, controls, and observable evidence. It should make the safe path the fast path: registered system identity, approved model alias, evaluation suite, policy-bound tools, accessible components, trace and cost schema, launch packet, and rollback. Allow exceptions through an explicit review with an owner and expiry. Measure golden-path adoption, time to first evaluated prototype, lead time to bounded production, exception age, duplicated infrastructure, and platform support load. A path nobody uses is documentation, not a platform product.

Stage autonomy by evidence and consequence:

Stage Authority Evidence to advance Reversion condition
observe offline replay; no user effect representative task value and failure taxonomy corpus or population no longer representative
advise read-only recommendation comprehension, accessibility, reliance, outcome lift failing slice or misunderstood scope
act with approval proposed effect; explicit human authorization reviewer discrimination, reversible receipts, stable economics rubber stamping, stale state, or review debt
bounded autonomy allowlisted low-consequence effects outcome SLO, invariant controls, recertified risk treatment burn, drift, invariant breach, or material change
broader autonomy larger scope with independent oversight sustained evidence across novelty, cohorts, and failures any gate outside approved residual risk

Advancement is not a maturity badge. Different task classes can remain at different stages indefinitely. Reversion must be technically cheap and organizationally legitimate. An agency budget from Chapter 1 caps authority; good product evidence can spend more of that budget, but cannot erase an irreversible consequence or a weak control.

Track review debt: decisions awaiting independent challenge, expiring exceptions, controls without owners, untested recertification triggers, stale model cards, unsupported custom infrastructure, and launch conditions temporarily waived. Like technical debt, it accumulates interest as staff and system context change. Give every item principal, owner, due date, compensating control, and repayment test. Do not turn “risk accepted” into an immortal status.

Also track human capability. Heavy assistance can deskill reviewers, narrow situational awareness, and make fallback impossible. Rotate work, sample unassisted performance when ethical, rehearse degraded modes, provide feedback on caught and missed cases, and design career paths that do not reduce domain experts to exception handlers. Monitor workload, interruption, emotional burden, exposure to harmful content, decision pressure, appeal conflict, and control over pace. Chapter 27 measures the queue; organizational leadership owns whether the job is sustainable and whether incentives reward careful dissent rather than approval volume.

28.11 Operate AIMS through every material change

An AI management system (AIMS) is the repeatable organizational machinery for policy, objectives, roles, resources, risk treatment, operation, evidence, audit, corrective action, and improvement. It is not a document folder and not a one-time launch council. Chapter 25 established governed safety claims and regulatory interfaces. Here, the organization turns them into an operating loop.

Use the NIST AI Risk Management Framework functions as a useful risk lifecycle: Govern establishes culture, policy, roles, accountability, and oversight across Map, Measure, and Manage. Map establishes context, intended purpose, affected people, dependencies, impacts, and risk tolerance. Measure selects and interprets evidence. Manage prioritizes treatment, decides whether objectives are achieved, accepts bounded residual risk through authorized roles, and monitors the decision. These functions are iterative, not a checklist sequence.

Wrap that risk work in plan-do-check-act. Plan policy, objectives, scope, risk method, competence, and controls. Do the controlled development and operation. Check performance, incidents, audits, complaints, accessibility, reliance, outcomes, and control effectiveness. Act on nonconformities, root causes, changed context, and opportunities to improve. Management review supplies resources and resolves cross-team accountability; it does not rubber-stamp a packet.

Begin with an inventory entry that can answer: system and owner; intended and prohibited uses; users and affected parties; autonomy and effects; models, prompts, tools, data, vendors, regions, and interfaces; outcome and guardrail metrics; risk tier and rationale; approvals; evidence bundle; deployment population; monitoring; incident and appeal path; and recertification triggers. Inventory identity must join the release bundle and operational telemetry. A spreadsheet with no release hash cannot tell which governed system ran.

Risk tier determines a minimum review path. Consequence, affected scale, reversibility, vulnerability, rights impact, data sensitivity, novelty, autonomy, external exposure, and uncertainty are common inputs. A product can raise its review rigor; it cannot lower a required tier by calling an effect a “recommendation.” Document the rationale and independent challenger. Keep the full rubric in the organization’s governance standard rather than embedding another ad hoc scorer in application code.

A risk-register entry should state hazard, cause or scenario, affected parties, consequence, likelihood or uncertainty, existing controls, control evidence, residual risk, treatment, owner, due date, and status. Connect hazards to product conditions. “Overreliance” becomes a measurable scenario: new reviewers accept wrong categories; the control is an evidence-first review design; the evidence is the four-cell cohort matrix; the gate is a maximum wrong-accept rate. “Accessibility” becomes a task-completion scenario, not a generic promise.

Every material change passes through the loop in Figure 28.4. Model aliases, system prompts, tool schemas or descriptions, retrieval corpora, data policy, write scope, deployment region, affected population, vendor, interface, and risk controls can invalidate old evidence. Define triggers before change, resolve them against an immutable bundle diff, and require the appropriate delta evaluation. A typo need not rerun every study, but the owner must justify the affected evidence. Periodic recertification catches drift that no discrete release declares.

The launch review is a decision record, not a presentation. It names scope, evidence period and population, baseline, unresolved uncertainty, failed and passed conditions, residual risks, accountable owner, independent reviewer, decision, permitted autonomy, expiry, rollback, monitoring, and recertification triggers. Conditions should be machine-checkable where possible and human-judged where necessary. The code evaluates four quantitative gates but does not pretend to automate risk tier, residual-risk acceptance, professional judgment, or accessibility diagnosis.

# launch_metrics.py — exact gate-evaluation core
limits = fixture["kill_criteria"]
max_cohort = max(
    item["overreliance_rate"] for item in reliance["by_cohort"].values()
)
assistive_completion = funnel["by_cohort"]["assistive_technology"][
    "conversion_from_previous"
]["completed"]
checks = (
    ("appropriate_reliance", reliance["overall"]["appropriate_rate"],
     limits["minimum_appropriate_rate"], ">="),
    ("cohort_overreliance", max_cohort,
     limits["maximum_cohort_overreliance"], "<="),
    ("assistive_completion", assistive_completion,
     limits["minimum_assistive_completion"], ">="),
    ("cost_per_outcome", economics["cost_per_outcome"],
     economics["baseline_cost_per_outcome"], "<="),
)
return [
    {"gate": name, "observed": observed, "threshold": threshold,
     "passed": observed >= threshold if operator == ">=" else observed <= threshold}
    for name, observed, threshold, operator in checks
]

When a condition fails, do not average it into an overall readiness score. Choose among repair and repeat, narrow population, lower autonomy, add a time-bound compensating control, continue a non-effectful pilot, or retire. Record who can approve each choice. The fixture permits read-only use only for experienced reviewers while the team repairs the interface and repeats cohort evaluation. That is a conditioned product decision, not an operational incident response.

Internal audit asks whether the management system is implemented and effective: samples can be traced from inventory to bundle, evidence, decision, operation, and recertification; owners exist; exceptions expire; and corrective actions close. Management review asks whether objectives, resources, competence, performance, complaints, audits, changes, residual risks, and opportunities require a policy or investment change. Both need independence proportionate to consequence.

Now assemble the launch-review packet using the Appendix B template. Keep it to a decision surface, with detailed evidence linked rather than pasted:

  1. Product claim. For internal expense reviewers, recommend a category and write it only after approval; reduce cost per correctly realized categorization relative to the conventional workflow.
  2. Scope. Candidate release, pilot business unit, reviewer populations, one reversible write, explicit prohibited uses, measurement window, and baseline.
  3. AIMS record. Inventory identity, risk R-EXP-017, high tier and rationale, hazards, owners, independent reviewer, controls, residual uncertainty, and seven recertification triggers from the fixture.
  4. Reliance plan. Four-cell correctness source, overall and cohort gates, confirmed catches, decision-time interpretation, minimum sample policy, and the interface changes to test.
  5. Accessibility plan. End-to-end tasks, access modes, standards baseline, manual and participant testing, completion gate, failure diagnosis, owner, and remediation evidence.
  6. Value and economics. Baseline, funnel, attribution method, qualified outcome, cost components, break-even review time, distribution of labor, and fixed environmental boundary.
  7. Decision. Record the three failed conditions and one passed condition. Hold full launch and the write action. Permit only the read-only experienced-reviewer pilot while repairing the evidence-first and assistive-technology interaction.
  8. Expiry and next review. Name the change bundle, sample and duration required for repeat evaluation, decision owner, independent challenger, rollback, and date after which the exception expires.

Do not “fix” the exercise by changing thresholds or deleting the cohort. Propose at least two interaction variants: one that shows the evidence and proposed write before the category recommendation, and one that requires an initial category judgment on high-uncertainty cases. Predict effects on all four reliance cells, active review time, assistive completion, and cost. Then specify the powered comparison in Chapter 22 terms and the exact evidence that would restore write authority.

The packet is complete when another reviewer can reproduce the counts, trace every condition to its predeclared owner and response, distinguish measured fact from judgment, and understand why the economically attractive candidate is not authorized for full launch. Chapter 32 reuses that conditioned-decision discipline in the capstone review rather than inventing a new launch process.

28.13 What endures, what changes

What endures. Treat an agent as a socio-technical product with user, product, and organization evidence. Make goal, plan, evidence, effects, and receipts legible. Optimize appropriate reliance rather than acceptance. Make accessibility an end-to-end correctness property and gate slices. Test a causal outcome against the least agentic baseline. Follow the funnel past invocation, price qualified outcomes including review and remediation, and state environmental boundaries. Give a stream-aligned team outcome ownership, a platform paved road, independent challenge, staged autonomy, review-debt accounting, and sustainable human work. Operate inventory, risk, evidence, launch decision, monitoring, audit, and recertification as one AIMS loop.

What changes. Interface conventions, model capabilities, agent frameworks, commercial pricing, environmental measurements, benchmarks, accessibility guidance, laws, vendors, standards editions, and certification practices will change. The dated Landscape box quarantines those claims. Removing it leaves the launch argument, equations, organizational boundaries, and conditioned decision intact.
